Project Brief


3.7 Million homes are broken into each year. Home security is such an important topic but still needs improvement, which is what interested our team to further look into this topic for our University project. As a result our team, JJS, decided to create an autonomous drone, that would be feasible in the next 10 years. Our project brief is to â€œimprove the efficiency of home security and reduce the number of home invasions".

Our Design


Most burglaries happen between the times of 10am-3pm. This is usually because people are at work. Empty houses make for easy targets for burglars. But what if your house never looked empty though? What if it never became a target for home invasion? What if it there was a system that could be used to deter burglars not only through alarms and lights but also through making the house seem as though there are people in it?  Terry is a drone that has been created to do all of those things with the intention of providing a more effective means of home security, stopping the burglar even before they get into the house. Features

By replicating a presence in the home, the house wouldn't look empty, and as a result wouldn't be a target for intruders. This was achieved by linking the drone to speakers and lights to make replicate someone being in the house.

From our previous designs, the idea of surveilence was touched up. We decided to add the feature of surveillance not only to keep the house more safe but to also catch anyone in the act of intruding.

Following our idea of scaring the burglar, we found that alarm systems work best in deterring home invasion. As a result an alarm feature was added to the drone.

Design Process


Research
Firstly our team decided use questionnaires to gather a wide range of opinions and understand how most people view think in regards to home security. We then followed up with interviews to gain a deeper understanding of, not only people who may have been burgled, but also common strategies used by burglars.

Idea generation
We used brainstorming to come up with a basis for some of our ideas. Once we knew which direction to go in we decided to start using the 6-3-5 brainwriting method to further refine our ideas. We then chose the best idea and improved upon it, even adding features from the previous ideas.

Low Fidelity Prototype
We then came up with ideas for low fidelity prototypes that were visually portrayed through the use of storyboards. These ideas were then created and tested through contextual observation, think aloud method, and A/B testing. Through testing we found which concept would work best.

High Fidelity Prototypes
Our team decided to make two interfaces, an app and a smart speaking system. We decided to focus more on the app as the user feedback proved that the app was more effective than the smart speaking system. We also used the feedback to further improve the app. We saw that aspects such as the map, community and the lack of a tutorial were all matters that needed to be attended to. After these issues were resolved we produced a product that we believe effectively improves the home security of a user.
